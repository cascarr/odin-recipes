Nkwobi Delicacy

Nkwobi is a delicious Nigerian spicy cow feet delicacy. It is a popular eat out meal common in Nigerian restaurants and bars. At first, it might seem complicated to make but in reality it's quite easy to prepare, with simple ingredients too.

Ingredients

  • 2500g of cow foot
  • Salt/bouillon cube to taste
  • 1/2 cup of palm oil
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons of grinded cameroon pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of grinded ehuru (calabash nutmeg)
  • 1 cup of ugba (optional)
  • 3 Tbs of coarsely grinded crayfish
  • 1 large onion divided (half chopped up, the other 1/2 sliced in circles)
  • Sliced Utazi leaves (for garnishing)

Steps

Method 1

  1. Season the cow feet with ginger, garlic, bouillon, and chopped onions. Add water to a level that covers the meat and begin to cook. Make sure to add more water when needed as you cook.
  2. Cook the meat till tender, then turn off the heat and let it cool.
  3. When the meat is cool enough to touch, cut it into bite sizes and don't discard the bones.
  4. Put back the bite size meat and smaller bones into the pot, add cray fish, some bouillon powder, and about 1/4 cup of water. Turn the heat back on, but low.
  5. Pour palm oil into a bowl and set it aside.
  6. Mix baking soda in a tablespoon of water, then pour the mixture into the oil and mix. Stir unti mixture thickens to form a paste.
