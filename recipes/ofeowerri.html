Ofe Owerri Delicacy

Ofe owerri was made popular by an Igbo song which made it known that one can only afford it if only that person is rich / wealthy. This is due to the high quality and quantity of meat and fish use in the preparation of ofe owerri.

But really, one doesn't have to break the bank to prepare this special delicacy from ala Owerri, Imo state Nigeria! A simple budget can do, if carefully planned.

Ingredients

  • Assorted fish
    • Dry fish
    • Stock fish
  • Assorted meat
    • Beef
    • Snails
    • Cow skin (ponmo)
  • 10 corms medium cocoyams
  • 1/2 cup of palm oil
  • 1/4 cup of ground crayfish
  • Habanero pepper (to your taste)
  • 4 small seasoning cubes
  • 1 medium bunch of ugu (Nigerian pumpkin leaves)
  • 1 small bunch of ukazi (Afang leaves, Gnetum africanum)

Steps

  1. Set the boiled meat on the cooker. The quantity of water in the pot should be just a bit less than the level of the meat and fish.
  2. Add crayfish and dry fish. Cover it and leave it to boil.
  3. When it boils, add the palm oil and cocoyam paste (in lumps). Cover it and continue cooking on high heat.
  4. Once the soup thickens, take off the undissolved cocoyam paste (if any).
  5. Add the chopped peppers and vegetables.
  6. Cover it and once it boils again, add some salt if necessary.
  7. Stir very well and transfer the soup to a cool container immediately so the veggies will remain green.
